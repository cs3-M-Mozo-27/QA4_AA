Biomedical Engineering Applying Engineering Principles to Healthcare and Medicine

What does a biomedical engineer do?

Biomedical engineers work across a diverse range of fields. They design innovative medical equipment, enhance medical imaging technologies such as MRI and CT scans, develop prosthetics and assistive devices for people with disabilities, research diagnostic systems, manage health data, and integrate medical technologies into clinical environments for more healthier and safer health towards all.

When did biomedical engineering begin?

The formalization of biomedical engineering as a discipline occurred during the 1950s and 1960s in response to medical technology and healthcare advancements. Recognizing the necessity to integrate engineering principles into medicine for improved patient treatment, and care, there was a growing impetus to establish dedicated academic programs in biomedical engineering, despite prior instances of collaboration between engineers and medical professionals.

The Future of Biomedical Engineering

Innovative medical devices emerging from biomedical engineering labs globally have revolutionized how physicians address disease and trauma, significantly improving the quality and duration of human life.
